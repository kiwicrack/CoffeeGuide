Curious about how coffee is processed?
Coffee processing refers to the way that a seed is removed from a coffee cherry.

There are various methods to remove the seed from that cherry,
and these methods affect the seed's flavor as it gets roasted and turned into a coffee beanâ€”this flavor sticks around till the final brew.
There are four different ways to process coffee, all of which change the sweetness, body, and acidity of brewed coffee.


The widely popular Washed Process will be outlined below!







SORTING


———


Coffee cherries are firstly picked and sorted by ripeness and density respectively using a sorter.




DEPULPING


———


A massive amount of water goes into the machines that removes the outerskin and pulp of the coffee cherry.

The mucilage though, is still left on the seed and is processed in the next step.




WASHING & DRYING


———


The seeds are washed of the left over mucilage on them before they are dried.

After washing, the coffee seeds are dried in the sun on drying beds.




ROASTING & BREWING


———


The dried coffee beans are packaged then sold to consumer.

Consumers would typically roast their coffee beans in their preferred ways to get a certain type of flavour.

After that, they would then grind the roasted beans and brew them.
